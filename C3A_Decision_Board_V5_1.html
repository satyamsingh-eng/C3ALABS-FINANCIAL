05 — SarvaX Landing Page

What works and what to change

What is working
"AI that executes" is genuinely differentiated. Most AI answers questions — this positions SarvaX as action-taking.
Wealth Advisory in the agent dropdown signals a real vertical focus, not generic AI.
90-day deployment story creates a concrete sales anchor.
The B2B solution/agent structure feels intentional, not generic.
What to change
"Not assist, not suggest, not draft" + "run autonomously" — reads as no human needed. Financial buyers will not trust this.
Agent status labels must be honest — active / demo-ready / coming soon. Never show unbuilt agents as live.
Enterprise security claims need proof. If there is no SOC 2 yet, say it is in process.
Wealth Advisory should be framed as the first wedge — not the whole company story.

Change: "AI that executes"
To: "AI that executes approved workflows"
Adding "approved" is not a qualifier that weakens the message. It is the feature that makes it enterprise-sellable.

Change: "run autonomously" / "SarvaX handles the rest"
To: "runs with review, approval, and control"
Financial services buyers need to see governance, not autonomy.

07 — Why Attractive

What pulls us in

They have money

Tech spend is 3–5% of revenue. Advisors pay $150–300/seat/month for tools that save time. The price point is proven.

They already get GenAI

85% of advisors call GenAI helpful (Advisor360, 2025). You don't need to explain why AI matters.

Work is repetitive

The same 8-step post-meeting workflow, hundreds of times a year. Perfect for agent execution.

Meeting and document heavy

Exactly what KaraX/CORE already handles. Almost no new infrastructure for the core use case.

Mistakes are expensive

Manual errors in compliance notes or missed action items have real consequences. ROI is obvious.

Compliance creates urgency

$600M+ in SEC FY2024 recordkeeping penalties. CCOs and managing partners have board-level pressure to improve documentation.

One workflow becomes many

Annual review → all reviews → onboarding → client calls. The same architecture scales across every client interaction type.

08 — Why Dangerous

What can hurt us

Compliance is unforgiving

One incorrect claim to a CCO or regulator and credibility is gone. There are no second chances in this market.

Trust takes time

Advisors are personally liable. They will not adopt a new tool without seeing it work on real meetings first.

Integrations must be real

Promising CRM push and failing to deliver kills the demo. Verify Wealthbox / Redtail connectors before any meeting.

Jump is moving fast

$105M raised, 27,000+ advisors, #1 in advisor satisfaction. Now building toward an "AI operating system." The window is real but narrow.

No room for fake autonomy

Any claim of "no human needed" will be challenged immediately. Approval governance is a feature, not a limitation — lead with it.

Security claims must be proven

A financial buyer will ask for the SOC 2 report and the BAA template in the first week. 404 on /security kills the conversation.

One wrong claim kills credibility

FINRA Ready on a pricing page, unlinked cert badges, "no human in the loop" — any one of these can end a deal before it starts.
